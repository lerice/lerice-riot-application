My League Experience

I go by the name of lerice playing in Oceania's Summoners Rift, after I left my past identity of LovelyNuts behind when migrating from North America. I've been an active player ever since the start of season 1, with so many great memories from my first pentakill with Master Yi to playing countless hours of ranked 5's throughout the night, to my personal greatest achievement, reaching Diamond earliar last year. Me and my close group of friends were so dedicated and enjoyed the game so much as to even having a brief stint in a sponspored team (although that didn't last long), as well as placing top 16 in the Oceanic Season 3 online tournament. We were literally 2 wins away from competing at PAX Melbourne but ended up being blown away by our opposition.

I achieved gold in Season 1 and 2 (forever flaunting my gold season 1 summoner icon!), and finally broke into platinum near the start of Season 3 after being stuck at 1800 elo for a rough period. Following the brand new opening of Oceanic servers in 2013, I was able to climb through the (arguably easier) competition and achieve Diamond during late Season 3. Although I may have slowed down my playing pace as of recently (as time allows me), I am nearing ever so closely to my 1000th normal game victory, a statistic that I may not take the most pride but belongs to me.

My Passion

I love watching people do what they are good at. I love watching pro players play the game, be it League of Legends, Dota 2, osu!, Legend of Zelda or any game. I love e-Sports, and gaming in general for that matter. I love learning from example, be it a player flaunting to the world a brand new champion or build competitively, or even as easy as reading a guide on how to play a champion I've never played before. I love theory-crafting and trying to maximise an output with minimal input, be it trying to get as much DPS as possible on an AD carry (sorry, Marksman) with ~12k gold or stacking as much CC as possible in a team composition without undesirable lanes. I love playing games and I play to be the best that I possibly can, never willing to settle for second best.

Most importantly, all these passions of mine don't only apply to gaming. I've been PC gaming of one sort or the other (MMO-RPG's to RTS's to MOBA's) nearly all my life and can't imagine living in this day and age without them. The thought of working with a big gaming company on something I am this passionate about has always tickled my pickle (not in the sexual way...), but nothing I have really had any huge thought about. Until now. Enter first-year university, where I am studying a whole range of units to try and figure out what I really enjoy doing. Out of the blue, something appeals to me. Programming.

Not any kind of programming. Good programming. Clean programming. Efficient programming. The thought that I can literally construct something from scratch, complete it and have it function properly had me astounded me and hooked. From simple working programs like bank account simulators to tic-tac-toe to dictionaries, I thoroughly enjoyed programming. Then finally I started working more with websites, and have never looked back.

Working at Riot as a Web Technology Intern (or perhaps a Software Engineer - if I'm qualified!) would be one of my greatest passions, not only allowing me to be closer to a part of e-Sports, something I love, but I would be doing something I enjoy doing (web design!), with the people I want to work for the most (you guys!) - and that is almost too much to be asking for.

I don't mind many personal preferences (such as bracketing single line if-statements, or having white space around equals signs), as long as through-out the code it is consistent and therefore more readable :D. Even in my HTML code I try to make as easily readable as possible. Please do have a look at some of my source code and contributions on my GitHub.

    I am also a big believer of convention over configuration in software/coding, which is why I believe Ruby (on Rails) to be the greatest language to have in my pocket when applying for this position, as well as overall being the perfect framework for me to use to develop web applications. Lastly I would love to highlight my ability or desire to complete work fast and on time, I find that when I have my mind set on a job I will work to get it done ASAP, and have always found myself managing deadlines and time constraints with ease.
