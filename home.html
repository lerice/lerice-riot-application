My League Experience

I go by the name of lerice playing on Oceania's Summoners Rift, after I left my past alias of LovelyNuts behind when migrating from North America. I've been an active player ever since the start of season 1, with so many great memories from my first pentakill with Master Yi, to playing countless hours of ranked 5's throughout the night, to my personal greatest achievement, achieving Diamond in solo queue earlier last year. Although I may have slowed down my playing pace as of recently (as time allows me), I am nearing ever so closely to my 1000th normal game victory, a statistic that I may not take the most pride but will forever be mine.

My Passion

I love watching people do what they are good at. I love watching pro players play the game, be it League of Legends, Dota 2, osu!, Legend of Zelda or any game. I love e-Sports, what Riot is doing to promote e-Sports, and gaming in general for that matter. I love learning from example, be it a player flaunting to the world a brand new champion or build competitively, or even as easy as reading a guide on how to play a champion I've never played before. I love theory-crafting and trying to maximise an output with minimal input, be it trying to get as much DPS as possible on an AD carry (sorry, Marksman) with ~12k gold or stacking as much CC as possible in a team composition without undesirable lanes. I love playing games and I play to be the best that I possibly can, never willing to settle for second best.

Most importantly, all these passions of mine don't only apply to gaming. I've been PC gaming of one sort or the other (MMO-RPG's to RTS's to MOBA's) nearly all my life and can't imagine living in this day and age without them. The thought of working with a big gaming company on something I am this passionate about has always tickled my pickle (not in the sexual way...), but nothing I have really had any real thought about. Until now. Enter first-year university, where I am studying a whole range of units to try and figure out what I really enjoy doing. Out of the blue, something appeals to me. Programming.

Not any kind of programming. Good programming. Clean programming. Efficient programming. The thought that I can literally construct something from scratch, complete it and have it function properly had me astounded and hooked. From simple working programs like bank account simulators to tic-tac-toe to dictionaries, I thoroughly enjoyed programming. Then I eventually transitioned that enthusiasm into working more with websites, and have never looked back.

Working at Riot as a Web Technology Intern (or perhaps a Software Engineer - if I'm qualified!) would be one of my greatest passions, not only allowing me to be closer to a part of e-Sports, something I love, but I would be doing something I enjoy doing (web design!), with the people I want to work for the most (you guys!) - and that is almost too much to be asking for.
